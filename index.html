Choropleth Map

The choropleth map on the left shows the Global CO2 Emissions per Capita in 2016, by mapping the data of each country onto a world map. Some country without data is mapped with grey colour. According to the map on the left, the continents North America and Oceania have high CO2 emissions compared to other continent as they have darker colour.

Bubble Plot


On the left shows the relationship of CO2 Emissions VS Population of Different Countries. As we can see, the higher the population, the higher the CO2 Emissions of the Country. China, which has the highest population among our dataset, has also the highest CO2 Emissions.

Donut Chart


Donut chart on the left shows the Yearly Change of CO2 Emissions of Different Countries. Asia has the highest yearly change of CO2, which means maybe Asia has more activities such as burning of oil, coal and gas, as well as deforestation, compared to other continent. Positive values means the CO2 Emission is increasing while negative values means the CO2 Emission is decreasing.
