GLOBAL CO2 EMISSIONS

Chan Yu Anne




Recently, global climate has changed due to many factors such as human activities that release large number of carbon dioxide (Ritchie & Roser, 2020). As we can see, North America and Oceania have high CO2 emissions compared to other continent as they have darker colour. This means that North America and Oceania might have more human activities such as deforestation. Some country without data is mapped with grey colour. Qatar has the highest CO2 Emission which is 37.29 tons.






As we can see, the higher the population, the higher the CO2 Emissions of the Country. China , which has the highest population among our dataset, also has the highest CO2 Emissions. This might be caused by higher population means more human activities. Besides, we can also find out the relationship of Carbon Dioxide Emissions, Population and Life Expectancy by looking at the boxplot below the bubble chart. The continent Europe has higher life expectancy compared to other continent. Looking back to the bubble plot, looks like Europe has lesser countries compared to continents such as Asia and Africa, and Europe's Carbon Dioxide Emission is also lesser. Thus, we can say that continent with lesser Carbon Dioxide Emission has higher life expectancy .



Asia has the highest yearly change of CO2, which means maybe Asia has more activities such as burning of oil, coal and gas, as well as deforestation, compared to other continent (Kolich, 2021). Some continents are splitted into two parts as one indicates the positive yearly change and one indicates the negative yearly carbon dioxide change. Positive values means the CO2 Emission is increasing while negative values means the CO2 Emission is decreasing. As a conclusion, we should decrease the carbon dioxide emissions so that our mother earth is healthy and we can have higher life expectancy too.
